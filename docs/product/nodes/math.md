# Math nodes

Math nodes combine and **shape control signals** (and sometimes audio) with **arithmetic and logic**. They appear under **Math** in the **Catalog**. Use them to scale modulation, build logic, pick between sources, or clamp extremes before they hit sensitive inputs.

## Math

**Arithmetic** on two or more inputs: add, subtract, multiply, divide—depending on the operation you choose in the UI. Core tool for scaling LFO depth, offsetting envelopes, or mixing CV-style values.

## Compare

**Comparison** operations (for example greater-than, less-than). Outputs a control you can use to **switch** behavior or drive a **Switch** node.

## Mix

**Blend** between two values with a crossfade or mix amount—smoothly move from one modulation source to another.

## Clamp

**Limits** a signal to a minimum and maximum so wires never push a parameter past safe or musical bounds.

## Switch

**Selects** one of several inputs based on a **control** or index—useful for preset-like routing or rhythmically changing sources without repatching.
