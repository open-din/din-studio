# Effects nodes

Effects process **audio**: dynamics, tone, space, modulation, and analysis. They appear under **Effects** in the **Catalog**. Chain them between **Sources** and **Routing** nodes.

Typical patch order: **Gain staging** → **EQ / filter** → **dynamics** → **modulation** → **time-based FX** → **analyzer or metering** (optional) → **mixer / output**.

## Gain

**Level** and **trim** in the simplest form. Use to balance branches before summing.

## Filter

**Tonal shaping**: low-pass, high-pass, band-pass, or related modes depending on implementation. A prime target for **LFO** or **envelope** modulation.

## Compressor

**Reduces dynamic range** so loud and quiet parts sit closer—glue for drums, bass, or full mixes.

## Delay

**Echo** with feedback and time controls. Modulate delay time for tape-style or chorus-like effects.

## Reverb

**Room and space**—from small rooms to large halls. Often fed through **aux sends** instead of inserting on every channel.

## Phaser

**Phase-shifting** sweeps; classic psychedelic and jet textures.

## Flanger

**Short delayed copy** mixed with dry signal for hollow, jet-plane motion.

## Tremolo

**Amplitude wobble** at low rates; can also suggest gated or chop textures depending on depth and waveform.

## EQ3

**Three-band equalizer**—quick broad strokes on low, mid, and high.

## Distortion

**Saturation and waveshaping** from gentle warmth to aggressive clipping.

## Chorus

**Detuned copies** for thick, moving width on keys, guitars, or pads.

## WaveShaper

**Custom or preset waveshaping** for harmonic distortion and aggressive tone design.

## Convolver

**Convolution reverb or creative FIR** using an **impulse response** from the **Audio Library**. Long IRs add space; short IRs add speaker or body character.

## Analyzer

**Visual spectrum or level** feedback so you can see—not only hear—energy in the signal.

## Pan

**Stereo placement**—static or modulated left/right position.

## Panner 3D

**Spatial audio** placement for wider or game-style mixes when you use height/azimuth-style parameters.
